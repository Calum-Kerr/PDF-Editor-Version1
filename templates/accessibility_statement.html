Accessibility Statement

Last updated: 29 March 2025

RevisePDF is committed to providing a website that is accessible to the widest possible audience, regardless of technology or ability. We aim to comply with all applicable standards, including WCAG 2.1 (Level AA).

1. Measures Taken

We have taken the following measures to ensure accessibility:

  • Implementation of semantic HTML structure
  • Proper labelling and descriptions for interactive elements
  • Sufficient colour contrast throughout the site
  • Clear and consistent navigation
  • Keyboard accessibility for all functionality
  • Text resizing without loss of functionality
  • Alternative text for all meaningful images
  • ARIA landmarks and roles where appropriate
  • Regular testing with assistive technologies

2. Compatibility with Assistive Technologies

RevisePDF is designed to be compatible with the following assistive technologies:

  • Screen readers (including JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver, and TalkBack)
  • Speech recognition software
  • Screen magnification tools
  • Alternative input devices

We test our website regularly with various assistive technologies to ensure compatibility and to identify areas for improvement.

3. Compliance Status

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) defines requirements for designers and developers to improve accessibility for people with disabilities. It defines three levels of conformance: Level A, Level AA, and Level AAA.

RevisePDF is partially conformant with WCAG 2.1 level AA. Partially conformant means that some parts of the content do not fully conform to the accessibility standard.

4. Known Limitations

Despite our best efforts to ensure accessibility, there may be some limitations:

  • Some PDF documents within the site may not be fully accessible. We are working to provide accessible versions of all documents.
  • Some third-party content that appears on our website may not be fully accessible.
  • The preview functionality for PDFs may have limitations with screen readers. We provide alternative ways to access this functionality.

We are continuously working to address these issues and improve the accessibility of our website.

5. Accessibility of PDF Tools

At RevisePDF, we not only strive to make our website accessible but also provide tools that help users create more accessible PDF documents:

  • Our accessibility checker helps identify accessibility issues in PDF documents
  • Our tagging tools help users add proper tags to make documents more accessible
  • Our OCR functionality can extract text from images to make content readable by screen readers

6. Feedback and Contact Information

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of RevisePDF. Please let us know if you encounter any accessibility barriers:

Email: calum@revisepdf.com
Based in Scotland, UK

We try to respond to feedback within 3 business days.

7. Formal Complaints

While we strive to maintain an accessible website, if you encounter problems that cannot be resolved, you can file a formal complaint.

In the UK, website accessibility for public sector bodies is enforced by the Equality and Human Rights Commission (EHRC) or the Equality Commission for Northern Ireland (ECNI).

Although RevisePDF is a private company, we are committed to the principles of digital accessibility and take all reasonable complaints seriously.

8. Updates to This Statement

We will regularly review and update this accessibility statement. As we improve our website and implement new technologies, we will update this statement to reflect these changes.

This statement was last updated on 29 March 2025.
